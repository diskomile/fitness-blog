Building muscle as a skinny person can be a challenging and frustrating process. However, with the right approach, it is possible to gain weight and build muscle mass. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive guide on how to build muscle as a skinny person, including the best nutrition and training strategies.

## Understanding Your Body Type
As a skinny person, you likely have a fast metabolism, which means you burn calories quickly. This can make it difficult to gain weight, but it also means you have a high potential for muscle growth. To build muscle, you need to focus on consuming more calories than you burn, and providing your body with the necessary nutrients to support muscle growth.

## Nutrition for Muscle Building
Nutrition plays a critical role in building muscle. You need to consume a calorie-surplus diet that provides your body with the necessary protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats to support muscle growth. Aim to consume 1.6-2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight, and focus on eating complex carbohydrates such as brown rice, sweet potatoes, and whole grain bread. Healthy fats such as nuts, seeds, and avocado are also essential for hormone production and overall health.

Aim to eat 5-6 meals per day, spaced out every 2-3 hours, to keep your muscles fueled and support growth.

## Training for Muscle Building
Training is also crucial for building muscle. Focus on compound exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press, which work multiple muscle groups at once. Aim to train each major muscle group 3-4 times per week, and use progressive overload to challenge your muscles and stimulate growth.

## Supplementing Your Diet
Supplements can also be helpful in supporting muscle growth. Protein powder, creatine, and mass gainers are all popular supplements that can help you meet your nutritional needs and support muscle growth.
